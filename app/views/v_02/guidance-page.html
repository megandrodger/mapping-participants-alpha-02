What are the digital inclusion categories?

  • they are nine digital inclusion categories that range in a scale from ‘never have, never will’ to ‘expert’
  • they are intended to capture all digital skills, non device or web specific
  • they are used inorder to help ensure that services are being designed for all digital confidnece and skills
  • in a service assessment researchers will need to proof that they have conducted research with lower inclusion categories (service dependent)

How to decide which digital category a research participant

During a research session you must draw out information about the participant that helps you place them in different categories. Below are a list of questions and activities that you could chose from to incorporate into a research session that will help expose confidence or skills.

Activity
Send your participant their consent form attached to an email. In the session ask them to download, fill it out, and attach it to an email to send back.
This could be helpful if your service doesn’t involved any complex interactions. Though a lot of users are comfortable with email, the understanding of downloading, and finding it on the computer to attach it might expose a lack of confidence, or hesitation.

Question
Will you talk me through how you use your phone, day to day?
Though this question is device specific, if the user mentions apps, online banking or listening to podcasts it might help understand their attitude towards technology.

Question
How much do you use your computer; is it mainly for work, social arrangement, or entertainment?
If the user works with computers at their work, they may be comfortable with specific skills but how they use their technology in their free time might help decide between ‘task specific’ and ‘basic skills’ categories

Activity
Ask your participant to save a bookmark, and then return to it later in the session.
Using this would be dependent on what your service is, where it might work is if there is any source of information that the user might be able to refer to, or if there are different sections of the prototype that they could switch between.

Activity
Ask your participant to change a setting on their browser
For example to increase the text size in the page, or to use a different browser. This may reveal the difference between someone who is comfortable trying a new digital activity or someone who has a lower level of confidence
